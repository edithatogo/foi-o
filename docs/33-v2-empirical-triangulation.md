# V2 empirical triangulation

FOI-O V2 develops process concepts through a continuing inductive-deductive
cycle. Seed concepts may come from observed requests, platform vocabulary,
legislation, guidance, parliamentary materials, review findings, expert domain
knowledge, or computational extraction. No origin is automatically privileged.

A codebook concept progresses through `seed`, `pilot`, `adjudicated`,
`validated`, and `stable` maturity only after definitions, inclusion/exclusion
rules, positive/negative examples, source alignment, reviewer agreement, and
error analysis are documented. Individual assertions separately carry
`observed`, `inferred`, `asserted`, `certified`, or `unknown` epistemic status.

The new empirical contracts are overlays. They link to existing FOI-O events,
ledger entries, trace spans, lineage records, legal sources, annotations, and
gold cases. They do not replace those structures.

## Existing rule runtime

The existing `foi_o_nz.oia_rules` implementation is the first concrete
rule/process coupling consumer. V2 does not replace it. It adds independently
curated, time-versioned empirical evaluation and trace requirements so that
deterministic output remains reproducible, contestable, and correctly bounded
as a candidate result.

## Platform states

Raw FYI and Right to Know states are source evidence and remain immutable. Their
relationship to candidate process events is a versioned empirical mapping,
reviewed against correspondence and attachments and monitored for platform/time
drift. A source state cannot by itself certify a legal outcome.
