# Evidence and derivability

Evidence is represented by independent dimensions:

- authority: binding law, authoritative interpretation, official
  administration, platform record, participant account, or model output;
- directness: direct, corroborative, contextual, or inferred;
- integrity: hash-verified, archived-unverified, live-unverified, or derived;
- temporal applicability: event-time match, retrospective context, prospective
  context, or unknown;
- visibility: public, restricted, internal-required, or unknown.

A summary display grade is optional and cannot certify a claim.

Derivability assessments list the facts required for a claim, facts observed,
facts missing, allowed outputs, prohibited outputs, and the most conservative
status: directly derivable, partially derivable, attachment review required,
agency internal record required, human legal review required, or not derivable
from the public platform.
